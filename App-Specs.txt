2) Theme A — Regulatory & policy pull: formal networks, guidance, and new decision points
A1. Europe: Federated RWE at scale (DARWIN EU) + new HTA gates (JCA)

Closed claims: high capture, longitudinal, but latency ~3 months and less payer/account detail;
Open claims: higher volume, lower latency (~1 week), but fragmented and noisier;
EMR: richer clinical detail + notes, but lower coverage and heavier analytics burden;
Lab: biomarker and testing pathways, but incomplete coverage and vendor gaps. [2025 RWD D...References | PowerPoint], [biopharmin...tional.com]

The same internal deck explicitly describes “emergent properties” when linked (e.g., EMR + claims adds ‘why’ to ‘how much’; labs + claims support cost‑effectiveness and outcomes‑based contracting), which mirrors broader industry movement toward evidence built from multi‑source, cross‑validated signals. HTA bodies and evidence evaluators increasingly push RWE toward prespecified causal questions and explicit bias handling. NICE’s framework updates explicitly add references and tools to support target trial approaches, and expand discussion of external validity bias and adjustment for differences between study sample and target population. C2. External control arms (ECAs) are rising—but remain contentious
Industry commentary points to ECAs becoming more mainstream in clinical research and a driver of efficiency. Unmeasured confounding (severity, functional status, socioeconomic factors)
